What You Will Learn (12-Week Path)

Week 1 – DAW Setup & Workflow (Ableton Live)

Installation, template design, and essential organization tools. We'll set up your DAW for maximum efficiency: project templates, markers, sample browser shortcuts, and CPU optimization. You'll learn track organization, routing basics, and how to structure sessions for creative flow. By the end, you'll have a personalized template that saves time on every project and keeps your workflow clean and fast.

Week 2 – Drums, Rhythm & Groove Foundations

Kick and snare selection, drum layering techniques, and programming human-like grooves. We'll explore sample processing, swing and microtiming, and how to build rhythmic patterns that lock with the bassline. Learn to layer kicks for power and clarity, design fills that maintain energy, and create variations that keep listeners engaged without feeling repetitive.

Week 3 – Kick & Bass Relationship / Sidechain

Frequency interaction between bass and kick, dynamic separation techniques, and creative sidechain compression. We'll dive into bass design fundamentals, sample layering, and how to make both elements coexist cleanly. You'll master sidechain automation for punch and depth while keeping the low-end defined and club-ready.

Week 4 – EQ & Compression Fundamentals

Practical equalization across different tracks: surgical EQing, high-pass filtering, and creative frequency shaping. Learn different compressor types, attack/release timing, ratio selection, and how to chain processors for clarity. We'll apply these tools to drums, bass, and effects busses with real-time feedback and critical listening exercises.

Week 5 – FX, Space & Atmosphere

Creating depth with reverb, delay, and modulation effects. Learn how to use effects tastefully to place sounds in space without muddying the mix. We'll cover automation techniques for risers, sweeps, and transitions that build energy and maintain movement. You'll understand effect sends, return channels, and how to design ambient layers.

Week 6 – Melody & Basic Harmony

Melodic construction within electronic music frameworks. Explore scales, intervals, and simple harmonic progressions that complement techno basslines. Learn to create memorable hooks, transform simple ideas into full phrases, and integrate harmony without cluttering the arrangement. Practice melody-to-arrangement integration with hands-on examples.

Week 7 – Sound Design Basics (Synths & Samplers)

Introduction to subtractive synthesis, FM, and wavetable synthesis. Learn oscillators, filters, and envelopes to create custom sounds. Explore sampler manipulation, time-stretching, and creative resampling. We'll design leads, pads, and bass sounds from scratch, then integrate them into your tracks with your own sonic identity.

Week 8 – Arrangement & Structure

Building a complete track from loops and ideas. Learn intro, build, peak, and outro structures suited to techno. Master the art of tension and release through strategic additions and removals. We'll analyze reference tracks, create arrangement templates, and develop decision-making frameworks that prevent overproduction while maintaining momentum.

Week 9 – Transitions & Dynamics

Creating smooth section transitions using automation, effects, and creative editing. Learn how to manipulate energy between sections for maximum impact. Use risers, breakdowns, and silence as compositional tools. Practice micro-editing and rhythmic builds that feel natural and exciting in a live DJ context.

Week 10 – Mixing Fundamentals

Gain staging, level balancing, and panoramic placement for clarity. Learn bus architecture, group compression, and how to maintain consistency across the mix. Explore parallel compression, mid-side techniques, and checking your mix in mono. We'll establish a workflow for mixing that translates to different playback systems.

Week 11 – Track Finalization

Critical review and optimization of arrangement and mix. Learn to identify and remove unnecessary elements that cloud the sound. Practice final automation passes, stem organization, and preparing your track for mastering. We'll discuss alternative versions (radio edit, extended remix) and how to deliver professional files to external collaborators.

Week 12 – Export, Loudness & Release Preparation

Export settings, file formats, sample rates, and mastering preparation. Understand LUFS targets and loudness standards for streaming platforms. Learn metadata, artwork requirements, and distribution strategies. We'll discuss pitching to labels, building your release calendar, and using feedback for continuous improvement in future tracks.
